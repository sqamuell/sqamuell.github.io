Design Fabrication

Ariane Et Pasiphae was painted in 1935 by an artist and architect Le Corbusier. The subject of Ariane Et Pasiphae is two women, a mother and daughter, the greek goddesses Ariadne and Pasiphae. They look up to the moon on the right side of the image. Below it, a bull stands. The painting is one of the many Purist paintings of the time. Purism, a variation on cubism had a large influence on french art and architecture. It is denoted by simple shapes void of detail and strong colors that imply the subject through masterful composition. The scenes in Purist paintings tend to read as very flat, as space is not clearly visible but is rather implied through use of color and proportion.

The investigation of the painting began with a series of analytical steps in order to break down the composition. Proportion and color were analyzed in order to find the implied spacial qualities of the painting. These served as the baseline for interpreting the spacial qualities that were explored through the following series of models and drawings.

This project was designed & fabricated in Spring 2019.
